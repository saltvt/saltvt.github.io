Vtuber Opsec Basics
**Date:** 2026-04-14
**Subject:** [[Shorts]] [[Opsec]] [[Security]]


As someone who's been on the internet for a bit of time, I've seen a lot. So today we're going to give you guys some basic tools to protect yourself on the net. These are just precautions and anyone with enough resources, time, and will power can find you. The whole point of this guide is to make the level of difficulty not worth it. I'm not going to get super technical in this post but I will give you some standard operating procedures in order to keep yourself safe. I'll do a more advanced and in depth guide that tackles AI, Social Engineering, and being compromised at a later date.

# Identity
-  <b>NEVER</b> post your full name, Age, or Location on the internet. This may seem obvious to a lot of people but it still happens a lot. 
-  Your username/persona should be something unique and something you've never used before
-  Check to make sure everything you post does not have uniquely identifying information
- I know Vtuber vs. IRL is a controversial topic but I'll say this. Do not post photos of your face on the internet unless you are certain you have good Operational Security. Personally, I don't like posting my face. Anytime I have the photo was taken 3-6 months before or even longer. Make sure to keep identifying locations and objects out of the photo as well
# Account Security
- Separate your personal email from your Vtuber Email. New accounts. New Names. Your Personal and Work emails should never be associated with your 
- Authenticator/Physical Keys/2FA
	- There are a couple of really good password managers and phyiscal hardware devices that I would recommend (Bitwarden, 1pass, YubiKey, etc.). Writing down your passwords and saving them in plain text is the worst thing that you can do. Every password should be saved in an encrypted file or in a password manager. 
- Operating Systems
	-  You should have a separate windows/Mac/Linux account for Vtuber activities on your computer just for general purposes and to avoid doxxing yourself in Discord(screen share) or on Stream
	-   Location Data Should be turned off in the settings of your Operating System!!! This tends to be the easiest way people accidentally doxx themselves.
# Digital Footprint/Data Security
- Every 2-3 months make sure to google yourself/persona in order to get an understanding of what your presenting to the internet. This affects sponsorship and business opportunities as well. 
- I'll go into Data Broker Sites and the pitfalls of them at a later time. Just know that anything you post online adds to your digital footprint. Your inferred identity is also a part of this but staying vigilant and intentional with what you post is the best way to avoid having to 
- Regularly Delete Videos that you feel don't represent you as a creator or person. Most websites have a rolling 30-60 day wait time for the data to be actually deleted. This doesn't protect you from scraping or stalking so make sure to keep on top of the body of videos, photos, and text you post online. 

# Community Management

- Boundaries
	- This is important in any community to state what types of behavior is and isn't allowed in your personal space as a vtuber or content creator
	-  The types of content that is/isn't allowed also needs to be stated. Categorizing what type of creator you are stops a lot of misunderstandings down the road.
- Moderation
	- You should have a couple of people that your trust as your moderation team.
	- Also having a set of rules when dealing with a Crisis. Whether that's doxxing in your community, harassment, racism, etc. This is very important when dealing with closed knit communities.
	- There are a whole host of behaviors that need to be watched for in communities and I won't be able to get to all of them but transparency, community investment, consistency in values, and acknowledgement of mistakes are what you are looking to foster in your community.
